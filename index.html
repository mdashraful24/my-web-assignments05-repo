Donate for Flood at Noakhali, Bangladesh

In 2024, Noakhali experienced devastating flooding due to continuous heavy rainfall and surging river levels. Over 2 million people were affected as homes, roads, and farmland were submerged. Seven municipalities were hit hardest. Schools were forced to close, and local authorities initiated relief efforts to provide support to the affected communities.

Donate for Flood Relief in Feni, Bangladesh

In August 2024, devastating floods hit Feni district in southeastern Bangladesh, displacing hundreds of thousands and inundating more than 100 villages. The floods, caused by heavy monsoon rains and water releases from upstream in India, inflicted widespread damage on homes, infrastructure, and agriculture. Over 350,000 people were left without electricity.

Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

In 2024, the Bangladesh quota reform movement reignited dissatisfaction with the civil service quota system, particularly the 30% reservation for descendants of independence war veterans. Led primarily by students and young professionals, protesters demanded a reduction in quota percentages, a greater emphasis on merit in job recruitment, and increased transparency. The movement gained momentum after a court ruling reinstated the quota system, which had been abolished following similar protests in 2018.
