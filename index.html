Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

In 2024, the Bangladesh quota reform movement reignited dissatisfaction with the civil service quota system, particularly the 30% reservation for descendants of independence war veterans. Led primarily by students and young professionals, protesters demanded a reduction in quota percentages, a greater emphasis on merit in job recruitment, and increased transparency. The movement gained momentum after a court ruling reinstated the quota system, which had been abolished following similar protests in 2018. The movement also showcased the power of civil society in Bangladesh, as students and young professionals took the lead in advocating for change.
